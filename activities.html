Kazakhstan Best Places to Go

Big Almaty Lake

Big Almaty Lake in Kazakhstan is considered one of the best places to visit due to its breathtaking natural beauty, stunning turquoise waters, and serene mountain surroundings. Located in the Ile-Alatau National Park, about 15 km south of Almaty, this alpine lake sits at an altitude of 2,511 meters and is fed by glacial meltwater, giving it its striking blue-green hue.

The lake’s changing colors throughout the year—from deep blue in summer to icy white in winter—make it a mesmerizing sight in any season. The snow-capped peaks and dense forests surrounding the lake create a peaceful and picturesque escape from city life. It is an ideal spot for hiking, photography, and nature exploration, offering panoramic views of the Tien Shan Mountains.

Additionally, the lake holds cultural and ecological significance, as it serves as a vital water reservoir for Almaty. While swimming is prohibited to preserve the purity of the water, visitors can enjoy the fresh mountain air, wildlife spotting, and scenic walking trails. The easy accessibility from Almaty makes it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

Bayterek Tower

Bayterek Tower is one of the most iconic landmarks of Astana, Kazakhstan, symbolizing the country's growth, aspirations, and cultural heritage. Standing at 105 meters tall, this futuristic monument represents a blend of traditional Kazakh beliefs and modern architectural design.

The tower's design is inspired by an ancient Kazakh legend of the Samruk, a mythical bird that laid its golden egg in the branches of the Tree of Life. The golden sphere at the top of Bayterek symbolizes this egg, representing prosperity, renewal, and the bright future of Kazakhstan.

Visitors can take an elevator to the observation deck inside the golden sphere, which is 97 meters high, marking the year 1997, when Astana (now called Nur-Sultan for a period) became the capital. From the top, breathtaking panoramic views of the city’s modern skyline, including government buildings and cultural landmarks, can be seen. A special feature inside the sphere is a golden handprint of former President Nursultan Nazarbayev, where visitors can place their hand as a symbolic gesture of unity and wishes for the country’s future.

Hazret Sultan Mosque

Hazret Sultan Mosque is one of the best places to visit in Kazakhstan due to its breathtaking architecture, spiritual significance, and cultural importance. As the largest mosque in the country and one of the biggest in Central Asia, it offers visitors a unique experience of Kazakhstan’s Islamic heritage blended with modern elegance.

The mosque’s grand white marble structure, intricate Islamic patterns, and massive domes make it a stunning landmark, with its main dome standing 51 meters high, the largest in Kazakhstan, and its four towering minarets reaching 77 meters each.

Ahmed Yasawi, a revered Sufi teacher, the mosque serves as both a place of worship and a symbol of Kazakhstan’s rich Islamic history. Visitors can admire the detailed craftsmanship, explore the beautifully decorated prayer halls, and experience the peaceful and serene atmosphere that makes it an ideal destination for both cultural exploration and spiritual reflection.

Kolsai Lake

Kolsai Lake is one of the most beautiful natural destinations in Kazakhstan, often referred to as the "Pearls of the Northern Tien Shan." Located in the Almaty region, this breathtaking lake system consists of three alpine lakes surrounded by dense forests, steep mountains, and lush meadows. The lakes are famous for their crystal-clear waters, which reflect the surrounding peaks, creating a stunning and serene landscape.

Kolsai Lake is an ideal destination for hiking, camping, horseback riding, and photography, offering visitors a chance to explore untouched nature. The area is home to diverse wildlife, including deer, bears, and rare birds, making it a paradise for nature lovers. The trek between the lakes provides spectacular panoramic views and a peaceful escape from the city.

Easily accessible from Almaty, Kolsai Lake is a perfect getaway for those seeking adventure, relaxation, or simply a chance to immerse themselves in Kazakhstan’s breathtaking natural beauty.

Charyn Canyon

Charyn Canyon is one of Kazakhstan’s most spectacular natural wonders, often compared to the Grand Canyon in the United States. Located about 200 km east of Almaty, this stunning canyon stretches over 150 kilometers and features dramatic red rock formations, deep gorges, and towering cliffs carved by the Charyn River over millions of years.

The most famous part of the canyon is the Valley of Castles, named for its unique rock formations that resemble ancient fortresses. The breathtaking landscapes make it an ideal destination for hiking, photography, and nature exploration. Visitors can walk along the canyon floor, where the towering cliffs create a surreal and picturesque environment.
